Why Data-driven decisions are very relevant to SME's

While I have worked with leading consultancies and major corporates for over a decade, my 5+ years at Peoplecert -an SME which has now evolved to a global player with over $1bn valuation- have taught me many things and allowed me to have an end-to-end business perspective.

During my time there, I realized that all the advanced analytical capabilities that I've seen and created in the Global 500 companies I had worked with, were not only relevant to PeopleCert, but helped the company make the choises that allowed it to become what it is now. During my time there I managed to:

  1. Increase Reporting Analysts' productivity 10-fold through Self-Service Analytics capabilities
  2. Establish a Data-Driven culture, which impacted the quality of decisions as well as operations (e.g., the timeliness and completeness of invoiced exams)
  3. Create a Team of Data & Analytics professionals from ground zero, with whom we’ve delivered:
    • The Enterprise Data Warehouse, which serves as the ‘single version of truth’ for decision-making
    • Automatic Reports & Advanced Analytics deliverables to:
      • Support strategic decisions through value stream monitoring & process mining
      • Inform business stakeholders through tactical sales reports across regions & product lines
      • Provide automatic P&L, budget monitoring and other financial reporting capability
      • Promote quality of customer service & online proctoring through KPI's & six sigma metrics
      • Monitor procurement & examinations processes through tactical & operational BI


    Maturity of data-driven decision making

    Generally speaking there are 4 levels of analytics maturity a company can achieve - and not so many companies have achieved to become optimized. Even companies in the Fortune Global 500 need to continuously invest to remain at the forefront of analytics.



    On the other hand, it is very easy for a company to progress from Level 1 to Level 2. With the technology we have at hand nowadays, it is very possible for an SME to progress to Level 3, generating value at each step along the way. Just like PeopleCert did!
