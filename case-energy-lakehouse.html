Case study

Data Lakehouse & BI reports

Fortune Europe 500 energy provider — Microsoft Fabric Medallion Architecture & Power BI

The challenge

One of Europe's 500 biggest companies needed to monitor the financial and operational performance of its growing e-mobility business — charging station utilization, availability and revenue — while also managing risk in the energy market. Their existing analytical assets needed to move onto a modern, scalable foundation without disrupting the reporting the business relied on.

What we did

✓

Led the migration to Microsoft Fabric, designing the Bronze and Silver Lakehouses and the Gold dimensional Data Warehouse, with source-to-destination mapping to control the data distillation process.

✓

Guided the team through the Databricks migration, expanding notebooks to record SCD Type 2 history in Delta Tables and building the SQL pipelines that populate the full dimensional warehouse, including SCD Type 6 dimensions.

✓

Built the semantic model and DAX measures underpinning the Power BI reports, empowering business analysts to run their own analyses.

✓

Developed Power BI reports on charging station performance, covering utilization, availability and revenue, with drill-through, bookmarks, role-based security and paginated reports for ad-hoc downloads.

✓

Established a data quality process, with automated controls and monitoring reports migrated into the new Fabric notebooks and pipelines.

The impact

3 Medallion layers migrated to Microsoft Fabric
1 consolidated view of e-mobility performance across markets
RLS role-based security and Power BI Apps for governed self-service
