Supply Chain Financial Planning & Analysis Marketing

Plan with confidence, not spreadsheets on faith

Example 1

Rolling forecasts and scenario modeling

An FP&A extension built on Power BI, such as Aimplan, lets finance teams edit forecasts, run scenarios and reforecast directly inside the reports they already use, instead of rebuilding budget models from scratch every quarter.

Decision it enables: which scenario — and which budget — to commit to, with the trade-offs visible before you sign off.

Example 2

Revenue, cost and margin monitoring

A single dashboard tracking revenue, cost and gross margin by month and by category replaces the month-end scramble to reconcile numbers across systems, and surfaces margin erosion while there's still time to act.

Decision it enables: which products, categories or stores to prioritize, discount or discontinue based on real margin, not assumptions.

Example 3

Self-service variance analysis

A well-modeled dataset lets business partners slice budget-versus-actual variance themselves in Excel or Power BI, on their own schedule, instead of filing a request and waiting on finance or IT to turn it around.

Decision it enables: faster, more frequent budget-owner check-ins on where spend is drifting from plan.
